What is Project27?

Project27 is a place for learning. The content draws on lived experiences and professional skills of the Project27 team. It is developed by a couple - Daniel and Danielle. Daniel is a scientifically-minded blind person, with an interest in social entrepreneurship. Danielle covers art, media, gaming, and self-advocacy, while expertly navigating the world of learning disability.

Our team and services help blind or learning disabled people to develop skills that matter. We help reduce frustrations caused by inaccessible learning tools, and build confidence in people's abilities. We do this through peer support, personalised opportunities for learning, creating and discovering new challenges in a safe place, built by it's community.

What we do

We tell our story. We teach and guide. We involve blind or learning disabled learners. We support you.

"Tell me and I forget. Teach me and I remember. Involve me and I learn." - Benjamin Franklin

Blog

The blog is a place where we tell our stories, and log the development of Project27. We want to share thoughts on blindness, learning disability, and other topics we find interesting. We do this to be the leading example.

Open the Blog

Guide

The Guide is a journal of life-long learning. It is a set of guiding notes, written with accessibility in mind. The notes cover topics that matter to us, let it be science, technology, or a matter of lifestyle.

Open the Guide

Volunteering

Volunteering is the way we involve learners of all abilities and skills. Through bespoke tasks and active learning, it is the volunteers who build Project27, while developing skills that matter to them. Together we learn, together we grow.

Open the Volunteering

Support

We know from experience, written tutorials and guides aren't always enough. Sometimes we just need a little more hands-on support. That's why we come to you, listen to your needs and unique circumstances, and why we want to help.
