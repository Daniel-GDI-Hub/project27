About Project27

This page introduces the Project27 vision and mission, why we do it, who we are, and where we want to be by 2050.

Meet the Project27 team

We are Daniel and Danielle, a couple from Brighton, England. We wanted to learn, so we started developing Project27. We live with disabilities, and we have a lot to share.

Learn more about the team

Where did Project27 begin?

Daniel wanted to collect his science notes from university and his family recipes all in one place. In addition, this library of notes had to be accessible for blind readers, like himself. The library had to be accessible regardless what device it is viewed on; offering the same level of user experience. This quickly turned into an exciting challenge of web development, and gave birth to Project27.

Welcome, my name is Daniel, and the Project27 vision is my vision too.

I lost my sight at the age of 16. Yet, I live a good life. I have a doctorate degree in science, I have a healthy social life, and I live independently. My journey was not easy, but it was doable with support and the right mindset. So now, I would like to help those who are at the beginning of their journey. This is why I created Project27.

Project27 is really a warehouse of challenges and solutions. I truly wish that the content and the spirit of Project27 will become not only my own, but a shared vision of many.

So long, all the best!
Daniel

What we do

We support people with learning disabilities and visual impairments, by creating a safe space and opportunities for learning.

  1. Active learning: We promote active learning, by getting volunteers involved with small projects to learn from, while they help with developing the Project27 site.
  2. Passive learning: We enable people to learn using accessible content, by producing a growing library of notes within the Project27 Guide.

We also support the families, friends, teachers, carers, and mentors of learners by inspiring and consulting.

  1. Sharing thoughts: We share how we see things with regards to sight loss and learning disability in our blog posts. We do this to lead by example, and inspire anyone who lacks the confidence to learn new skills.
  2. Offering support: Anyone who needs time to talk things through with us can book an appointment for a session of consulting, tutoring, or mentoring.

Whatâ€™s on Project27?

The site is split into two parts: Blog and Guide. Blog posts and Guide notes both come in two flavours: technical, and lifestyle.

The blog has two branches:

  1. LogBook - a technical blog to track challenges and milestones encountered during the development of projects.
  2. TWIST. - posts on the way we see things.

The Guide is what we refer to as the library or the journal. Initially, five volumes, separated into two collections, form part of the Guide.

  1. Science collection:
    • Mathematical and Physical Sciences (MPS); - a volume of notes on topics of mathematics and physics.
    • Computer Literacy; -a volume of notes on how to do small, practical tasks on the computer.
    • Statistics;- a volume of notes on how statistical calculations work in a mathematical sense.
  2. Lifestyle collection:
    • Cookbook; - recipes that we enjoy and for you to try out.
    • Vocal. - an album of short stories and audio recordings with family and friends.
